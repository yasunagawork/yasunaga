百笙 (hyakushō) - Centikhene

Centikhene is an electronic mouth organ that is modeled after the Laotian traditional mouth organ, Khene, and the Japanese traditional mouth organ, 笙 Shō. This is the most current electronic musical instrument project. Project page --> here. This project involved folloing skillsets;

  • circuit design and schemetic drawing
  • PCB assembly
  • 3D modeling and 3D printing
  • mechanical design
  • woodworking
Interms of circuit design, below are the breakdown of the design componetns in this project.

  • Opamp ciruits
  • CMOS logic gates
  • voltage regulator circuits
  • pre-amplifier circuit
  • speaker amplifier circuit

Project has gone through few iterations of prortotying, iterating through design, layouts, materials, and archtecture. Image below from left, 1) handcarved copper board 2) cnc machine milled copper board on hand crafted case 3) Printed circuit board manufactured

Project is designed in a circuit design software Osmond and KiCad. Prototypes are tested through breadboard >> handsoldered boards >> manufactured PCB. Images below are PCB manufactured.

Version 1 of manufactured PCB
Version 2 of manufactured PCB

I have adopted the simple wind sensor that are developed by Ian Fritz and iterated to be bidirectional. LED light shines onto LDR, this triggers notes with the movement of the diaphragm both at inhale and exhale, allowing the player to play rhythmically and continuously. 3D model of wind sensor Here. Voltage from the wind sensor is scaled and shifted by op-amp and then gates the oscilators, alowing them to make sound when we breath in or out.

This project was contracted by Ye Qin Zhu for grant project for Yale School of Medicine. I have worked on a circuit design and constructed the board that houses transistror circuit for each hight power LED lights, a microcontroleler, and a infrared sensor. I have programmed the microcontroller for the light fading sequence that is cordinated with the presence of a person.
