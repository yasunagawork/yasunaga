PEER REVIEW

Everyone is responsible for giving anonymous written feedback for each project. This feedback will be turned in at the end of class and factored into the final evaulation of the each project.

I will redistribute the feedback after Spring Break, so please write legibly and use individual pieces of paper.

Please address the following, giving a brief explanation for each:

  1. Does the project seem finished or successful as a fully functional prototype? (1-5 scale. 1: a lot of unresolved areas, 5: completely finished/functional) Explain:
  2. Overall, how successfully was the project realized? Did it seem to meet its intended goals? (1-5 scale. 1: not sucessful, 5: very sucessful) Explain:
  3. How well was the project built? Does it demonstrate thoroughness and attention to detail? Rate the project's craft (1-5 scale. 1: poorly built, 5: very well made). Explain:

  5. What is one area that you think could be improved?
  6. What overall grade would you give this project? Explain:
  7. Additional comments? (Optional)

PREPARE BRIEF PRESENTATION FOR PROJECT 2

The short presentation you give next week will consist of:

  1. Research & planning related to something you'd like to work on during the second half of this semester.

DETAILS

  • Total length: 5-10 minutes.
  • Format: Anything goes (Powerpoint/Google Slides, InDesign/Illustrator/Word document, webpage, video, stack of files) as long as the material is coherently organized! You must arrive to class prepared.
  • Tone: Casual but serious. Think hard about what you're showing, but you don't need to script everything out or use cue cards.
  • Due: Next week at 9am (Mandatory attendance. Don't be late!)

CONTENT

  1. What would you like to make next? You can be general or specific about this. Try not to think too hard about the future. What excites you? What's interesting? What seems enjoyable?
  2. Is this is a continuation of Project 1? If so, what are your plans to improve on or build upon the work that you have already done? Please focus on providing details relevant to the work you will be doing, not what has already been done and be very specific. (Examples: Production/playtesting schedule, development of concept art, restate/clarify project goals based on feedback from Project 1)
  3. Research interests: Provide at least 2 examples of things which represent current interests relevant to your second project. Think of this as background material. How do these inform your work?
  4. Research precedents: At minimum, you should show 2 examples of existing work (produced by someone else) which are directly related to what you'd like to make. Why these particular things?
  5. Anything else you think is important. Don't feel like you have to fill the full 10 minutes... you don't! :)
